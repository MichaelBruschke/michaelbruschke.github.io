Colour


Colour plays an important role in our products. When we use our brand colours in a consistent and recognisable way, people become more familiar with our brand. We also use colour to guide users in certain situations. (For instance, green to indicate success, red to indicate failure). Here you will find some general guidelines on use of colour in our products.

General

Though deciding which color to use depends on the situation, here are some general guidelines for using the chromatic palette hues:

  • Blue indicates a current or active state. It communicates management, progress, connectivity, or organization.
  • Green indicates success. It communicates save, create, add, available, done, approved, or resolved.
  • Yellow indicates that attention is needed. It communicates warning, pending, missing, or impeded progress.
  • Red indicates a problem. It communicates a critical state, destructive action, error, failure, removal, stop, or decline.
  • Grey indicates disabled. It communicates visible but unavailable, disabled.

Accessibility

Osirium are dedicated to continually improving the accessibility of our apps. When considering colour, we work for our apps to satisfy the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) Level AA.

  • Normal text must have a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1.
  • Large text is defined as 14pt Bold (18.5px) or 18pt (24px) and must have a contrast ratio of at least 3:1.
