Copy Style


Make your service sound like Osirium. By drafting copy that is conversational and centered on the user's task, you will deliver the exact information a user needs without cognitive overload.



Principles for successful copy

  • UX copy should be considered during the earliest phases of design and development. Don’t treat it as an afterthought.
  • UX copy isn’t written in stone. Test, improve, and evolve your product’s messaging over time or it will stagnate.
  • UX copy should be written for the customer. It’s less about listing “What we do” and more about painting a picture of “How we help improve your life.”
  • UX copy scrutinizes each word in an effort to maintain a brand’s tone. Poorly chosen words can jolt users from the expected brand experience.
