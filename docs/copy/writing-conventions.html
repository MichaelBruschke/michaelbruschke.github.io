Osirium Interface Design Guidelines Copy style

Writing conventions


Well-crafted words improve UX and provide people with decision-making clarity. The copy that Osirium presents users is a guiding voice that leads them through a product's most important interactions and infuses digital experiences with brand personality and cohesiveness.

Here's a quick list of the conventions we at Osirium have adopted.

Addressing the user

Speak directly to the user.

  • Address the user as "you".
  • Use "we" to refer to your own perspective. It's inviting and suggests we have a partnership with the user.
  • Don't use "I" or "me" to refer to the app's perspective.
Do Don't
Are you sure you want to withdraw your request? Withdraw this request?

Capitalisation

NEVER USE BLOCK CAPITALS FOR LARGE BLOCKS OF TEXT. IT'S HARD TO READ AND CAN BE INTERPREETED AS HOSTILE.

Always use sentence case, even in page titles and navigation items. Exceptions are proper nouns, such as:

  • PAM role titles (such as Owner, Admin etc)
  • Place names
  • device states (Known, Managed)
  • Brand names (such as Osirium, Microsoft)
  • Header cells in tables: Start time (*exception: PEM tables)
Do Don't
If both the 'Sync group' and 'Can start task' permissions are enabled, new users will be able to start tasks in PPA. Enable Session Recording and/or Require Change Tickets in the profiles.

Contractions

People are used to contractions in conversation, and they expect to see them. Avoiding them completely can make our products seem too formal and stuffy. However, certain contractions can cause confusion, especially to non-native English speakers.

Avoid should've, could've, would've and they've. Even though they save space, they're unnatural and and sound less conventional.

Avoid the ampersand (&) unless it's part of a proper noun (such as M&M's). It's a contraction of and per se, and.

Use conractions when they are a natural fit for the text.

Do Don't
You'll need to request access to access this device. Click the calendar icon to choose a time and then submit your request. You will need to submit an access request to access this device. Click the calndar icon, choose a time, & click submit.

Periods

Ending text with a period is appropriate for full sentences. Use periods in large blocks of text and do not use them for text shorter than a complete sentence.

  • Use periods at the end of full sentences in tooltips, error messages, dialogs and documentation.
  • Do not place periods on button text, radio buttons, labels or checkboxes.
Do Don't
Add one or more user groups and then add some member users. New user.

Accessibility and localisation

Our apps reach a wider audience if we wrote with localisation and accessibility in mind.

  • Avoid phrases that may not make sense to an international audience.
  • Do not use words that make assumptions about what the user can and cannot do.
  • Avoid unusual words when they aren't necessary. The more straight forward it is, the easir it is to localise.

Do
Withdraw your request?
You will need to submit a new request if you chnage your mind.
WITHDRAWKEEP

Don't
Cancel your request?
This will delete your request.
YESCANCEL
